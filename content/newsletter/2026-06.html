Hey everyone!

Here's what's been happening in our world this month.

Family

June was my birthday month. Had a blast celebrating with family and friends. I did enjoy it — but honestly, birthdays are weird for me. Or maybe I just can't put myself in a celebratory mindset.

I turned 35 this year. I don't expect to live to 100, but if I make it to 70, I'm right at the midway point — and I find myself looking back from that vantage. Excited to see what the future brings. One thing I know for sure: life is rich. It's full of unexpected surprises, turns, and reroutes. Day to day it can feel mundane or monotonous, but it takes looking back to realize how far you've come.

The weather's been getting lovelier, so I've been soaking up some quality time with the boys. They love the playgrounds and grabbing a snack here and there.

Professional

A good friend of mine basically pulled me back to Mongolia for two weeks. Through sheer perseverance, he's managed to organize the biggest tech event in the country — and he had me come give a talk. It was my first time speaking on a big screen like that. Needless to say, it was a great experience.

And damn, I got a pretty great photo out of it too.

Given the AI era we're in, I beefed up my gaming rig to run some local LLMs and image generation. The amount of processing power this stuff demands is massive (compared to a lot of things) — no wonder all the memory and chip stocks are skyrocketing. Whoever has the infra has the power to run everything. This space is going through a fascinating renovation.

I'm still on the hunt for that one idea that'll make me a millionaire. Lately I've been getting back into the IoT side of things — tinkering with electronics and sensors again.

Part of my home server build, I also set up a media service — still got some homework to do there. The past couple of months have been a bit light on media consumption, but here's some of what I caught (below).
